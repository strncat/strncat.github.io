<div class="stmt">
2.1: Problem 18: Show that if \(p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}\), then \(\left(\frac{p-1}{2}\right)! \equiv \pm 1 \pmod{p}\)
</div>
<!------------------------------------------------------------------------------------>
<h3>Proof</h3>
We know by Wilson's theorem that
<div>
$$
\begin{align*}
(p-1)! \equiv -1 \pmod{p}
\end{align*}
$$
</div>
Therefore, the only possible last digits are 0,1,4,5,6,9. $$\ \blacksquare$$
